Physics Quiz: Angular Momentum

Topic: Definitions, Conservation, and Systems

Student Name Grade 12 GS

Part I: Concepts & Calculation

Concept: σ, f, T

1. A solid disk with Moment of Inertia \( I = 2.5 ~kg\cdot m^2 \) rotates with a constant angular velocity \( \theta' = 4\pi ~rad/s \).

a) Calculate the Angular Momentum \( \sigma \):
\( kg\cdot m^2/s \) (Use π ≈ 3.14)
b) Find the Frequency \( f \):
\( Hz \)
c) Deduce the Period \( T \) for one rotation:
\( s \)
Formulas:
\( \sigma = I \cdot \theta' \)
\( \theta' = 2\pi f \)
\( T = 1/f \)
Concept: M (Torque)

2. A force \( F = 20 ~N \) is applied tangentially to the rim of a wheel with radius \( r = 0.5 ~m \).

Calculate the Moment of Force (Torque) \( M \).

\( N\cdot m \)
For a tangential force, Torque depends on Force and Radius: \( M = F \times r \).
Theory

3. The Theorem of Angular Momentum relates the rate of change of angular momentum (\( d\sigma/dt \)) to which physical quantity?

Analogy: Newton's 2nd Law states Force changes linear momentum. What changes angular momentum?

Part II: Conservation Principles

Internal Change of I

4. An ice skater spins with \( I_1 = 6 ~kg\cdot m^2 \) at \( \theta'_1 = 3 ~rad/s \). She pulls her arms in, reducing her inertia to \( I_2 = 2 ~kg\cdot m^2 \).

Step A: What is her initial angular momentum \( \sigma_{initial} \)?

\( kg\cdot m^2/s \)

Step B: Using conservation, calculate her new angular velocity \( \theta'_2 \).

\( rad/s \)
Angular momentum is conserved. \( I_1\theta'_1 = I_2\theta'_2 \).
System at Rest

5. A disk and a man are initially at rest. The man starts walking, gaining angular momentum \( \sigma_{man} = +12 ~kg\cdot m^2/s \).

What is the angular momentum of the disk \( \sigma_{disk} \)?

Start at rest means \( \sigma_{total} = 0 \). So \( \sigma_{man} + \sigma_{disk} = 0 \).
Variable Analysis

6. A particle rotates at radius \( R \) with velocity \( \theta'_1 \). The string is pulled until the new radius is \( r = R/3 \).

Given that \( I = mR^2 \), how does the new velocity \( \theta'_2 \) compare to \( \theta'_1 \)?

If R becomes R/3, then I becomes I/9. Since \( \sigma \) is constant, if I decreases by factor 9, \( \theta' \) must increase by factor 9.

Part III: Systems & Impulse

Inelastic Collision

7. Disk A (\( I_A = 4 ~kg\cdot m^2 \)) rotates at \( 10 ~rad/s \). Disk B (\( I_B = 6 ~kg\cdot m^2 \)) is dropped onto it (initially at rest). They stick together.

a) Calculate the total initial angular momentum \( \sigma_{system} \): \( kg\cdot m^2/s \)
b) Calculate the combined Moment of Inertia \( I_{combined} \): \( kg\cdot m^2 \)
c) Deduce the final common angular velocity \( \theta'_{final} \): \( rad/s \)
Total angular momentum is conserved. \( \sigma_i = \sigma_f \).
\( \sigma_f = I_{combined} \cdot \theta'_f \).
Impulse Theorem

8. A solid flywheel with Moment of Inertia \( I = 3 ~kg\cdot m^2 \) starts from rest. A constant external torque \( M = 15 ~N\cdot m \) is applied for \( \Delta t = 4 ~s \).

a) Calculate the final angular momentum \( \sigma_f \): \( kg\cdot m^2/s \)
b) Calculate the final angular velocity \( \theta'_f \): \( rad/s \)
1) Theorem: \( \Delta \sigma = M_{ext} \times \Delta t \).
2) Definition: \( \sigma = I \cdot \theta' \).
